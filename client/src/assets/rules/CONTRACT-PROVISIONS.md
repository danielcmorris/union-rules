# Contract Provisions

## 4.10 Holidays and Overtime

Double time applies on weekends and holidays. All work performed outside of regular scheduled working hours and on Saturdays, Sundays, and the following holidays shall be paid at double the regular straight time rate of pay:

- New Year's Day
- Martin Luther King Day *(California only)*
- Presidents Day *(Nevada only)*
- Memorial Day
- Fourth of July
- Labor Day
- Veteran's Day *(California only)*
- Nevada Day *(Nevada only)*
- Thanksgiving Day
- Friday following Thanksgiving Day
- Christmas Day

---

## 4.13 Eight-Hour Rest Period

If there is not an 8-hour break between shifts, the employee remains at PT.

When workers are required to work six (6) hours or more overtime outside of normal work shifts, they shall be relieved for a rest period of eight (8) or more continuous hours, or they shall be compensated at the appropriate overtime rate of pay for all hours worked until released from work for eight (8) or more continuous hours.

---

## 4.4 Show-Up Pay


All payment should be done in 2-hour increments until they hit 10 hours, at which point they should be paid for actual time worked.

| Hours Actually Worked | Hours Paid |
|---|---|
| Any time reported (up to 2 hrs) | 2 hours |
| More than 2 but less than 4 hours | 4 hours |
| More than 4 but less than 6 hours | 6 hours |
| More than 6 but less than 8 hours | 8 hours |
| More than 8 but less than 10 hours *(four-tens)* | 10 hours |
| 8+ hours (or 10+ hours on four-tens) | Actual time worked |

---

## 4.6 Minimum Call Out

When an employee is called out for unscheduled overtime work from home, he or she shall be paid for four (4) hours at the applicable rate of pay.

If the four (4) hours overlap into his or her regularly scheduled work shift, the straight time rate of pay for that shift shall begin at the end of the four-hour period and end at the regular quitting time.

---

## 4.7 Meal Periods

When an employee works overtime after the regular day or shift, or when called out to work at night, Sundays, or on holidays, the Employer shall provide all meals unless the employees complete the job on or before meal time.

- If the job is started not more than one (1) hour before the regular daily starting time, then, when employees are notified the previous day, no breakfast is to be provided by the Employer.
- When instructed before quitting time to report for the next regular daily starting time, employees shall provide their own lunch the same as on other days.
- When employees are required to work more than two and one half (2.5) hours past their regularly scheduled quitting time, they shall be entitled to a meal time as provided for in this Section.
- An employee will be entitled to a paid meal time at intervals of four and one half (4.5) hours thereafter (from the conclusion of the previously owed meal period) for as long as he works.
- In the event the Employer does not provide meals as set forth above, the Employer shall pay **$15.00**, and **one-half (½) hour pay** at the applicable rate, for each meal not provided.
- On storm damage only, Employer shall provide expenses for meals and lodging to employees after sixteen (16) hours.
- When employees are working storm damage and are sent from their regular show-up to another location, they will be provided meals and lodging until returned to their regular show-up.

## 5.2 Mileage — Subsistence


A flat rate of **$50.00 per day** shall be paid for each day worked.

---

## 6.11 High Time — Bonuses

**220KV Dead-End Bonus:** Any tension dead-ending of 220KV or higher voltage required to be done in the air on steel towers at any height shall require a bonus equal to the worker's straight-time rate. This bonus pay shall be at a minimum of two (2) hours and for each succeeding hour or portion thereof, shall be paid at the bonus rate.

> *Exceptions: This bonus shall not apply to hanging the dead-end on the towers after it has been made up on the ground. Soft dead-ends made without supporting the ladder from the conductor shall not be at the bonus pay.*

**75-Foot Height Bonus:** All workers required to work on any type of pole (lattice poles excepted) at the 75-foot level or higher shall receive a bonus equal to the workman's straight-time rate. This height will be measured from ground level to the point of attachment to the structure of the material or equipment being worked on.

**Aerial Basket Bonus:** Any work performed from an aerial basket suspended from a headache ball or hook shall also require a bonus equal to the worker's straight-time rate.
